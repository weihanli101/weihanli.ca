Adknown Inc.

Posted: 08/12/2017

Intro/Abstract:

This summer I was employed as a mobile games developer for Adknown. The company had recently started this branch and there was much room for growth and development. I was amongst five other coops that were employed to kick start this branch of the company. In this post I will outline my experiences, my challenges, and accomplishments while at Adknown.

What Adknown is All About:

Adknown is a web and app publishing company based in Guelph. They connect consumers and customers together by making targeting ads delivered through mobile apps, web publishing, web portals and more. They do this through analysis of Big Data and more recently adopted machine learning.

What I Set Out to Accomplish:

Given my position as a mobile games developer my goals included understanding and learning deployment across mobile platforms (iOS and Android). Originally the company was only making games for android, but knowing that Unity is well known for its cross capability I was sure that I would have a chance to deploy to iOS. Also knowing the programming environment was to be in Unity I wanted to take full advantage of this to exercise my object-orientated programming skills.

What My Job Was About:

My role in Adknown was a games developer. I worked closely with others that were recently recruited for the same position as I and we progress as a team developing mobile games. I worked with Git and its workflow. Exercised object-orientated programming daily, programming in C#. I deployed both Android and iOS applications. Elaborating more on iOS games they were deployed though unity cloud since the company had a shortage of apple devices which we then turned our iMac into a server to overcome that hurdle. I worked with unity’s lighting to create smooth and dynamic lighting. I wrote shaders to create camera effects and applied mathematical functions, theories, and vector math to develop dynamic visually appealing yet efficient mobile games.

Work, Academics and Computer Science:

Connecting my work term experience back into my academics it really fuels my passion to learn computer science. Seeing practical aspects to the academic labour is like seeing crops grow after you toil away at a field for times on end. I feel my experience at Adknown not only taught me new things that academics couldn’t offer but also taught me how to learn. A main topic from my academics that was consistently used was my algorithmic studies. I now see the absolute importance in algorithm design and how runtime is such a big factor in it. In university a run-time is just a number. However, in the workplace O(n) vs a O(n^2) run-time difference could mean 20-40 fps increase in performance.

Conclusions and Regards:

At the end of my work term I discovered that I had made the most games out of all the co-ops there. This gave me both inspiration and encouragement as I know I still have plenty of games ideas that I wish to release into the marketplace. I wish to continue to exercise creative, efficient, and elegant design whether in games or future projects whatever they may be. I also have now tasted the fruits of my academic labour and I hope in the future I will have a chance to exercise more of my academic knowledge.

Acknowledgements:

I liked to thank my co-workers and superiors at Adknown for providing me with the tools, opportunity and support to explore both my creative and academic side. It has truly been an inspirational work term and I hope to continue to develop mobile apps in the future.

Tulip

Posted: 01/07/2018

Intro/Abstract:

This fall I had the opportunity to work as a full-stack developer at Tulip. I was placed into the development tools team and worked hand in hand with some of the most brilliant minds that setup the backbone of the company’s entire infrastructure.

Digging into Tulip:

Tulip is a company that believes store associates of brick and mortar stores are key players in the success or failure of that store. They aim to equip store associates with the right tools to provide a thriving experience for both the associates and customers. Their application is tailored towards building associate and consumer relations. Tulip does all this through their mobile platform Tulip Retail.

My Role as a Full-Stack Dev:

My positon on the development tools team didn’t allow me to interact with Tulip’s platform in a direct manor. Instead I worked on internal tools made for developers that made developing the platform possible. I worked closely with the development operations team to write efficient and scalable code, a mentality they want to push towards all development teams. I participated in daily scrums and weekly sprints which solidified my role on the team. My work revolved around creating an interface for AWS. Simplifying complexity so that more time can be allocated towards developing the product rather than setting up back-end dependences for the product.

Academics and My Job:

Throughout my work term at Tulip I worked with multiple data structures, developed algorithms and had an extensive use of Git, required for team development workflow. These were skills I picked up from my academics which in turn was reinforced and expanded upon throughout the work term. Working at tulip made me realized the essential factor of having a good foundation of computer science. A foundation built up during my study terms. Without them I would have had a hard time stacking tremendous amount of new information piled on me every day.

Conclusion and Regards:

As my work term came to a conclusion I reflected upon what I accomplished and learnt. I learnt the importance of being throughout, being wary of edge cases, and the skill of debugging and reading other people’s code. I learnt that it is imperative that code is to be scalable and not just made to fit the task at hand. Having added these skills to my tool set I feel confident and better equipped for my next work-term wherever it may be.

Acknowledgements:

I would like to thank the dev-tools team at Tulip for providing me with a great experience, providing me with support when needed, and giving me a meaningful role to play in the company!

Left

Posted: 09/07/2018

Intro/Abstract:

TThis summer I worked as an Application Developer for Left. I was placed in charge of making a demonstration application for their API RightMesh.

About Left:

Left focuses bring connectivity to the world through a decentralize system. Where most technologies in our age today are client-server based Left focuses on P2P connections. Which can be seen in their latest project RightMesh.

My Role as an Application Developer:

My role in Left was to create a working android application that demonstrated their API from scratch along with documentations and reports about this API’s performance. The process took me through all levels of design and application. From prototyping and writing project proposals in the early stages of development. To designing and implementing decentralized data transfer protocols in the latter stages of development. The project took me from the frontend of the application such as designing the user interface then programming it out in xml. To the backend of the application where I had to architect and design how the data was store and retrieved.

Importance of Design:

Working at Left made me realize the important role that software design patterns played in creating software. Left also taught me to think outside the box when approaching problems. Up until now I have always worked with a client-server based mind set. Client requests information, server processes it and sends back a response. However, there are many things in decentralized systems that client-server side architecture will simply not work for. There were many days where instead of coding I spend the day thinking about how I should architecture certain features of the application. Left really made me realize importance in the saying “think before you speak” in my case “think before you code”.

Conclusion and Regards:

As my time at left game to an ending I reflected back and felt that I not only gain work experience but a new way of approaching problems. I learned that in a sense “all roads lead to rome” but some are just better than others.

Acknowledgements:

I would like to thank my supervisors Ben and Caryl for providing me with support when needed. As well as Jenna for helping me through UI/UX decisions!
